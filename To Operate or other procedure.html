    • Excessive bleeding needing blood transfusion with its risks, or re-operating to control blood loss;
    • Infection and/or inflammation of wounds and surgical sites;
    • Allergic reactions to medications, tape, and any material used during or after the surgery;
    • Injury to nerves of the mouth or face from the surgery and/or anesthesia site or blood vessels (veins-arteries), some needing further corrective surgery. These nerve injuries, in rare cases, may be permanent and/or painful and in many cases may have no successful corrective treatment;
    • An opening into the sinus that may require corrective surgery;
    • It is possible following or during oral surgery that a temporary or permanent “numbness” of the facial area, lip, chin, teeth, gums, and tongue could result and may be painful and permanent;
    • Jaw fracture or discoloration of the jaw disc could result requiring corrective treatment;
    • Entrance into the maxillary sinus may occur requiring additional treatment and in rare cases may result in chronic sinusitis;
    • Bruising, limited opening, damage to adjacent teeth existing dental implants, prosthetics, or restorations, vein inflammation discoloration of gums and facial skin;
