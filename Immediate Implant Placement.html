Immediate Implant Placement – Post-Op Instructions
Part 1 Immediate Implant Placement

You had an immediate implant surgery performed on you and you have a temporary crown screwed into the implant. This is a temporary crown and made smaller in size (as compared to final crown) so that it does not take the load of chewing and the implant can heal without being disturbed. The purpose of the temporary crown is to not let the socket collapse and you have no open wound after the surgery. There are usually no cuts or any stitches with this protocol.

  • Do not touch or try to feel the temporary tooth with your tongue or fingers.
  • Avoid eating on this side of the mouth to protect the temporary tooth/implant from accidentally taking the load of chewing.
  • Don't brush or floss around the temporary tooth for at least 1 month.
  • Irrigate the temporary tooth with stream of water salt or Perio-guard solution using a plastic syringe without hitting the temp tooth.
  • If you feel anything loose, please call us immediately.
  • Take antibiotics and pain killers as prescribed.
Part 2 Upper Jaw Implants with Sinus Lift Instructions

This treatment is performed to increase the height of bone available for your implant(s). It is imperative that you adhere to the following instructions carefully to maximize your healing and improve the long-term outcome of your dental implant(s).

  • Do not blow your nose for at least 7 days, as the pressure will delay or damage your sinus healing. If you have to sneeze, do not hold it back-sneeze out. Open your mouth and try to minimize the pressure in your nasal/sinus passages.
  • You may use nasal decongestants, such as over-the-counter Sudafed tablets or Afrin Nasal Spray, to keep your passages dry, especially if you have noticed continued drainage or feel the need to blow your nose.
