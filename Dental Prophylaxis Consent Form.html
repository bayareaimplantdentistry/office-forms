Procedure Dental Prophylaxis (Routine Cleaning)

Description

A dental prophylaxis is a preventive procedure performed to remove plaque, calculus (tartar), and stains from the teeth. It may include scaling, polishing, flossing, and oral hygiene instruction.

Benefits

  • Helps prevent cavities and gum disease
  • Promotes overall oral health
  • Removes surface stains for a cleaner appearance

Possible Risks & Side Effects

  • Temporary gum tenderness or minor bleeding
  • Tooth sensitivity to temperature or pressure
  • Rarely, infection or irritation of gum tissues

Alternatives

  • Delaying or declining treatment (may increase risk of dental disease)
  • More extensive periodontal treatment if indicated
