Consent : Tooth Whitening

Purpose

This consent form is intended to help you understand the benefits, procedure, and possible risks of professional chairside tooth whitening performed at Center for Implant Dentistry, Fremont. Please read carefully and ask any questions before proceeding.

Procedure

Our in-office whitening treatment uses a hydrogen peroxide-based gel designed to safely and effectively lighten the color of your teeth.

  • The gel will be applied in three (3) 15-minute sessions during your visit.
  • A cheek retractor will be used to keep your mouth open, and a protective barrier will cover your gums.
  • We will record your tooth shade before and after the procedure.

Our whitening system delivers maximum results in minimal time with careful control to reduce tooth sensitivity.

Possible Risks & Side Effects

While whitening is safe and effective, some patients may experience temporary side effects:

  • Tooth Sensitivity: Mild to moderate sensitivity can occur during or after treatment. Inform us if you already have sensitive teeth.
  • Gum or Soft Tissue Irritation: Occasionally, whitening gel may contact the gums or lips, causing slight irritation or whitening of the tissue. This typically resolves within 24 hours.
  • Existing Dental Work: Crowns, veneers, fillings, and bonding do not whiten and may appear darker after your natural teeth lighten. If needed, we can discuss replacement options to match your new shade.

Treatment Expectations

Results vary depending on your natural tooth color, diet, and previous dental history.

  • Some discolorations (e.g., from tetracycline stains, fluorosis, or deep enamel defects) may not respond completely.
  • No whitening result can be guaranteed.
  • You must avoid staining substances for 48 hours after treatment - including coffee, tea, red wine, tobacco, and foods with strong colorants (e.g., tomato sauce, curry).

Contraindications

Whitening treatment is not recommended for:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women
  • Individuals under 18 years of age
  • People with allergies to peroxide or similar ingredients
